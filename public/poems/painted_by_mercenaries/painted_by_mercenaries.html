Painted by Mercenaries

Mercenaries painted the walls I’ve been living in.
They painted these walls in their last days when the war came.
I still smell the bloodstains on the floor;
The water has a metallic taste that feels warm.
They built their last cage for a golden mind –

A mind that could hear the walls speak
And understand the hidden messages relayed in the colors they picked.
They also laid the foundation and layered the walls.

The architecture feels like something out of a safe haven,
Where birds can only squeak at the windows but never get in,
And crows can caw, but the sound will never get through.

Maybe these walls are bulletproof – I wouldn’t know;
I’ve never seen a gun. Or maybe I do know.
Perhaps these are thoughts from a past life.
When PTSD lay buried within, waiting for a quick awakening.
When the nights passed so fast, time felt like it was distilling.

But one thing I know:
The walls are speaking, the paint is drying, the ceiling is melting,
The mirrors are deflecting, the light is trapped in a prism,
The days are getting darker, and the nights turn into a bright pyramid.

I know there are things I don’t know.
I know I’m still too young to hope.
The architect of this home is archived within my soul.

I need to know. And I think I know the story,
But the memories are buried beneath.
Why am I in this home? Who built this dome?
Is it a prison to keep me safe or a home to keep me away?

I know the walls speak, but I never understood the language.
I know the paint is drying, but it’s still beautiful to me.
I know the ceiling is melting, but the liquid never stained me.
I know the mirrors are deflecting, but I still see my face on my screen.
The light might be stuck in a prism, but I’m stuck in it too.
The days are dark, but I’ve still got my glasses.
The bright pyramid isn’t the worst place to be.

We are all sand in this universe.
We still don’t know where we are.
We still don’t know how it came about.
I might be at war, or I might be at peace –
But home is always where I am, no matter the bloodstains beneath my feet.
